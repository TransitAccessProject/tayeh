We are a multidisciplinary team from Engineers Without Borders UofT advocating for free transit access for people experiencing homelessness. In 2015, the Toronto Poverty Reduction Strategy stated that reducing transit inequity is an essential part of reducing income inequality in Toronto. Equitable access to public transit means the ability to participate in and access economic opportunities, services, social and cultural networks. We believe that price should not be a barrier to this participation. This is why we are researching the effects of free transit on quality of life for youth experiencing homelessness. We want to create a base of knowledge displaying how access to these opportunities can positively impact quality of life. TAP for Youth is inspired by the “Routes to Homes” project led by Dr. Miriam Stewart in 2013 from the University of Alberta. Dr. Stewart’s project, also observing the effects of free public transit for youth experiencing homelessness on quality of life, led to a city-wide program of fully subsidized transit for all people experiencing homelessness in Edmonton. We at TAP for Youth are hoping to create a base of research to motivate such a program in Toronto. TAP for Youth is possible through our partnership and support from City of Toronto, TTC, and Metrolinx. We have active partnerships with two youth shelters in Toronto.

The Project

T.A.P for Youth is a proof-of-concept research project from Engineers Without Borders UofT looking to learn how free public transit affects safety, social inclusion, interactions with transit authorities, housing, and employment outcomes among youth experiencing homelessness.
