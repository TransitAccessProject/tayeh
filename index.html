The Transit Access Project for Youth (TAP for Youth)

is a proof-of-concept research project from Engineers Without Borders UofT looking to learn how free public transit affects safety, social inclusion, interactions with transit authorities, housing, and employment outcomes among youth experiencing homelessness. TAP for Youth possible through our partnership with the City of Toronto, Metrolinx, Horizons for Youth, and YMCA Sprott House.

Our Work

  • History:

    The project started in 2017, when the team was informed that Transit Access was a notable challenge for youth experiencing homelessness. The goal was to develop a project to provide any youth experiencing homelessness free transit if it was required.

  • Current Work:

    Currently, our project is on hold due to COVID-19. Our research will resume once it is again appropriate to promote transit use in Toronto. Until then, we are working to raise awareness about our work.

  • Next Steps:

    We are hoping to begin the distribution of the presto cards as soon as it is safe to do so.

Routes to Homes

Our study was inspired by the “Routes to Homes: Transit and Social Support Intervention for Homeless Youth” project conducted in Edmonton, Alberta, in 2013. The goal of their study was to analyse the impact of receiving public transportation assistance for youth experiencing homelessness. They found that the youth experienced a reduction in negative interactions with transit authorities, made them “feel normal” on transit, increased safety in some situations, and enabled youth to access services and supports beyond their normal walking range.

Routes to Homes: Transit and Social Support Intervention for Homeless Youth

This report examines what happened in Edmonton when youth experiencing homelessness were given access to transportation. When this intervention occurred the youth had fewer negative interactions with transit authorities, made them feel safer when using public transit and allowed youth to access more services around the city.
